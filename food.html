Delicious Foods in Goa



Pork Vindaloo

Derived from the Portuguese words for garlic (alho) and wine (vinho), this spicy Goan curry originated from a Portuguese sailor’s dish made with pork, garlic, and wine. Goan cooks substituted palm vinegar and added local spices.

Prawn Balchão

A spicy seafood dish made by pickling prawns in vinegar and spices. It’s a perfect blend of tangy, spicy, and sweet, served best with rice or bread.

Sanna

Soft, fluffy steamed rice cakes, often enjoyed with pork dishes or chicken curry. Sannas are similar to idlis but are made with coconut and fermented with toddy.

Goan Fish Curry Rice

A staple meal in every Goan household, made with fresh fish (usually mackerel or kingfish), coconut, and kokum. Served with rice, it’s the comfort food of Goa.

Bebinca

A classic Goan dessert with multiple layers made from coconut milk, sugar, egg yolks, and flour. It’s baked layer by layer and is rich in taste and tradition.
