About Goa

Goa, India's smallest state by area and the fourth smallest by population, is located on the western coast of India. Known for its beaches, places of worship, and world heritage architecture, Goa is visited by large numbers of international and domestic tourists each year.

Goa has a rich history of Portuguese colonization, which is evident in its culture, cuisine, and architecture. The state is divided into North Goa and South Goa, each offering unique experiences to visitors.

With its tropical climate, Goa has three main seasons: summer (March-May), monsoon (June-September), and winter (October-February). The best time to visit is from November to February when the weather is pleasantly cool and dry.
